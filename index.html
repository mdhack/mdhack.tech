About

Who are we?

Wootton Hack Club is a new club at Wootton High School under SGA review. Members will explore topics in different computer science fields such as game development, algorithms, open source software or other smaller code snippets. They will learn and communicate in order to freely create their own projects, and conduct researches related to the computer engineering field.

Mission Statement

Replicate the hacker culture & redefine hack!

Who can join?

Anyone! We will provide a free community for software developers and programmers to share their experiences and launch projects to collaborate, but we will also be a place for new programmers to get help from higher leveled ones.

Competitions?

Not really interested. We'll leave the challenges for the CCC. However, we will have a small group of people competiting in online hacking competitions like CTFs. Talk to Mingjie if you are interested.

CTF Team

What is CTF?

Capture the Flag (CTF) is a special kind of information security competitions. There are three common types of CTFs: Jeopardy, Attack-Defence and mixed. Jeopardy-style CTFs has a couple of questions (tasks) in range of categories. For example, Web, Forensic, Crypto, Binary or something else. (Adapted from CTFtime)
